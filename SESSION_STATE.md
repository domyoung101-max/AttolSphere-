### Calibration standard

- Formal, instrumented, and directly verified outcomes may support high confidence.
- Partial signals, directional hints, and live but unresolved developments must be treated as provisional.
- “Near-confirmed” is a staging label, not a resolution label.
- Suppression indicates temporary constraint, not final closure.

### Confidence discipline

- Do not assign 85%+ confidence unless the event is backed by explicit formal evidence or an equivalent hard trigger.
- Reduce confidence where prior sweeps showed persistent overstatement.
- Prefer calibrated probability over rhetorical strength.
- Treat diplomatic, ceasefire, and deadline-dependent events with heightened caution unless a formal instrument, statement, or deadline-failure makes the outcome operationally clear.

### Band governance

- 0–19%: reserved for highly unlikely outcomes or black swan branches.
- 20–39%: weak but real possibility.
- 40–59%: unresolved, contested, or materially contingent.
- 60–79%: strong but still not settled.
- 80–100%: only for formal confirmation, explicit instruments, or hard-date outcomes with direct evidentiary support.

### Sweep robustness rules

Each sweep must:

1. Re-check the latest factual state before carrying probabilities forward.
1. Separate confirmed outcomes from provisional ones.
1. Identify where the prior sweep overreached.
1. Recalibrate bands using observed error, not narrative preference.
1. Preserve sharpness where evidence is strong.
1. Widen uncertainty where evidence is fragile.
1. Carry forward only those confidence levels that survive the latest evidence test.

### Update logic

After each completed sweep: compare raw probabilities to resolved outcomes. Mark bands that were systematically too high or too low. Adjust the calibration map accordingly. Persist the revised map into the next generated session state. Do not freeze calibration — allow it to evolve with each sweep.

### Robustness constraint

The edition must remain analytically forceful without becoming overconfident. Robustness means better discrimination, cleaner confidence control, and fewer false certainties. The goal is not to flatten probabilities, but to make them behave more truthfully over time.

### Persistence rule

The updated calibration map shall be inherited by every subsequent session state. Each generation cycle must carry forward the latest corrected confidence mapping and apply it to the next sweep.

### Final standard

Favour calibrated accuracy over expressive certainty. If evidence is incomplete, say so in the probability itself. If evidence is formal and explicit, let the confidence reflect that directly.

### TQL — TRUTH QUALITY ASSESSMENT

Mandatory per AI-006. Every case, every edition. Exactly four items in this order.

**Item 1 — Key Assumption**
The single most load-bearing assumption the case analysis depends on.
Must state: the assumption AND the counter-assumption.

**Item 2 — Fragile Fact**
The single fact that, if wrong, would most materially change the hypothesis set.
Must state: the source tier AND the direction of impact if wrong.

**Item 3 — High-Impact Uncertainty**
The unknown that, if resolved either way, would produce the largest probability shift.
Must state: the shift directionally (e.g. “H-A1 falls to 55–65% if X”).

**Item 4 — Tier 4 Dependency Test**
Remove all Tier 4 sources. Does the dominant hypothesis still hold?
Answer must be YES or NO with one sentence of justification.

Omission of any item = deviation audit failure. Do not build PDF until all four items present in all five cases.

### GATES — DEFINITIONS IN FULL

**Gate 0 — Source Admission**
A source is admitted to analysis only if it can be assigned a tier (1–4) and a named incentive assessment. Anonymous sources require explicit Gate 0.4 rating. No source enters a fact table without both fields populated.

**Gate 0.3 — Absence Confirmation**
A claim that something did not happen must be confirmed across a minimum of three mandatory feeds. Mandatory feeds for kinetic events: CNN, NBC, Al Jazeera, Naval News. For Lebanon-specific: CNN, NBC, Al Jazeera, Jerusalem Post. Label confirmed absences as [FACT — absence observable]. If fewer than three feeds checked, label as [UNVERIFIED ABSENCE].

**Gate 0.4 — Confidence Rating**
Every source in a fact table carries one of: HIGH / MEDIUM-HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW. This is confidence in the source’s accuracy for this specific claim, not general credibility. Must account for incentive distortion at point of rating.

**Gate 0.5 — Cluster Single-Source Rule**
The following are treated as single sources regardless of how many outlets report them:

- AIS cluster: MarineTraffic, Kpler, S&P Global, Windward, UKMTO, LSEG shipping
- Iranian state media cluster: IRNA, IRIB, Mehr, WANA, PressTV, Fars, Tasnim
- Wire origination rule: Reuters and AP are originating wires. Downstream outlets citing them are not independent.
- Aggregator rule: Wikipedia and ACLED are downstream aggregators. Tier 4 only. Not independent.

**Gate 5 — Framing Revision Trigger**
If a disconfirmation threshold is crossed, the case must be reframed from scratch — not patched. The trigger condition must be stated explicitly in Part 5 of every case. Patching an existing frame after a Gate 5 trigger = structural error.

### HEURISTICS — DEFINITIONS IN FULL

**H1 — Incentive Analysis**
All named parties have incentives that may cause statements to diverge from intentions. Before assigning weight to any named-party statement, state the incentive and its direction. Named-party statement predictions require incentive analysis before C3 classification. LOW-MEDIUM confidence ceiling if no structural incentive identified.

**H2 — Anchoring Risk**
Prior probability estimates create anchoring bias in subsequent editions. Explicitly ask: if this were the first edition, what probability would I assign? If materially different from carry-forward, document the gap before proceeding.

**H3 — Beneficiary Asymmetry**
Identify who benefits from the current situation continuing. The beneficiary has structural incentive to obstruct resolution. Weight obstruction scenarios accordingly in the hypothesis set.

**H4 — Narrative vs Outcome Gap**
When official narrative and observable outcomes diverge, weight the observable outcome. Document the gap explicitly in Part 2. Do not resolve the gap by assuming the narrative is correct.

**H5 — Structural Contradiction**
When two stated positions are categorically incompatible, label as structural contradiction. Procedural gaps close with negotiation. Structural contradictions require one side to abandon a stated position — this is a higher bar.

**H6 — Suppressed Intersection**
When a single event has material probability impact on multiple cases simultaneously, it must be flagged in all affected cases. The intersection is suppressed when cases are treated as independent when they are not.
