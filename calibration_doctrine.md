# CALIBRATION DOCTRINE — ED27

## Purpose
This doctrine exists to correct systematic overconfidence, preserve analytical sharpness, and improve sweep robustness across successive editions.

## Operating principle
The generation pipeline shall apply the active calibration map to all raw forecast probabilities before publication.
Raw analytic judgment is not to be published as final confidence unless it already conforms to the calibrated standard.

## Calibration standard
- Formal, instrumented, and directly verified outcomes may support high confidence.
- Partial signals, directional hints, and live but unresolved developments must be treated as provisional.
- “Near-confirmed” is a staging label, not a resolution label.
- Suppression indicates temporary constraint, not final closure.
- Narrative momentum must never be mistaken for evidentiary closure.

## Confidence discipline
- Do not assign 85%+ confidence unless the event is backed by explicit formal evidence or an equivalent hard trigger.
- Reduce confidence where prior sweeps showed persistent overstatement.
- Prefer calibrated probability over rhetorical strength.
- Treat diplomatic, ceasefire, and deadline-dependent events with heightened caution unless a formal instrument, statement, or deadline-failure makes the outcome operationally clear.

## Band governance
- 0–19%: reserved for highly unlikely outcomes or black swan branches.
- 20–39%: weak but real possibility.
- 40–59%: unresolved, contested, or materially contingent.
- 60–79%: strong but still not settled.
- 80–100%: only for formal confirmation, explicit instruments, or hard-date outcomes with direct evidentiary support.

## Sweep robustness rules
Each sweep must do the following:
1. Re-check the latest factual state before carrying probabilities forward.
2. Separate confirmed outcomes from provisional ones.
3. Identify where the prior sweep overreached.
4. Recalibrate bands using observed error, not narrative preference.
5. Preserve sharpness where evidence is strong.
6. Widen uncertainty where evidence is fragile.
7. Carry forward only those confidence levels that survive the latest evidence test.

## Update logic
After each completed sweep:
- Compare raw probabilities to resolved outcomes.
- Mark bands that were systematically too high or too low.
- Adjust the calibration map accordingly.
- Persist the revised map into the next generated session state.
- Do not freeze calibration; allow it to evolve with each sweep.

## Robustness constraint
The edition must remain analytically forceful without becoming overconfident.
Robustness means better discrimination, cleaner confidence control, and fewer false certainties.
The goal is not to flatten probabilities, but to make them behave more truthfully over time.

## Persistence rule
The updated calibration map shall be inherited by every subsequent session state.
Each generation cycle must carry forward the latest corrected confidence mapping and apply it to the next sweep.

## Final standard
Favor calibrated accuracy over expressive certainty.
If evidence is incomplete, say so in the probability itself.
If evidence is formal and explicit, let the confidence reflect that directly.
